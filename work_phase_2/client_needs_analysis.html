Client Needs Analysis

The Client Brief

About Us

Our platform connects people through shared hobbies and subcultures, offering a space to explore, create, and engage. We ensure the experience is fun and inspiring, with an inclusive environment where everyone feels valued. Whether you're starting out or an expert, we celebrate passion and dedication. With clear guidelines and strong moderation, we maintain a safe and respectful space for all. Join us and connect with others who share your passions.

This brief has been prepared for the internal design teams at Connect Communities Initiative (CCI). Each design team is tasked with developing individual concepts and prototypes for dynamic websites that align with CCI‚Äôs mission and vision. Our mission is to strengthen societal bonds by creating platforms that celebrate and support the unique identities of communities across Australia.

Project Overview

We are commissioning the development of dynamic, user-friendly websites that represent and support different communities. Each website should serve as a central hub where community members can share resources, highlight events, and foster meaningful connections. The websites must not include any form of commercial activity, such as advertising or selling products. However, it is acceptable to promote resources, events, or services that are directly beneficial to the community.

The websites must be accessible, mobile-friendly, and reflect the diverse needs of the selected community focus area while adhering to our mission of inclusivity, accessibility, and ethical design. Each website should also celebrate the unique characteristics of its chosen community and support meaningful interaction and engagement.

Community Focus Area

  • Subculture and Hobbies Community

Additional Information

Target Audience

Each website must serve two key groups:

  1. Community Members: Individuals within the selected community who seek representation, resources, and connection.
  2. General Audience: Broader society, who can use the website to learn about the community and its contributions.

Accessibility must be prioritised to ensure seamless interaction for all users, including those relying on assistive technologies.

Project Goals

  1. Represent and celebrate the identity of the chosen community, recognising and representing the diversity within the community rather than relying on stereotypes.
  2. Provide clear, accessible information and resources to users.
  3. Foster meaningful connections and support within the community.
  4. Ensure ethical, inclusive, and culturally sensitive design principles are upheld.

Values - Needs - Features Analysis

Value 1: Playfulness

Card 1

Users need interactive features that make community engagement feel lighthearted and fun.

Feature

Gamified challenges or quizzes related to hobbies that encourage playful competition.

Card 2

Users seek playful challenges or activities to spark casual participation.

Feature

Regular hobby-themed events, such as virtual contests or themed weeks, to engage users.

Value 2: Inclusivity

Card 1

Users need a way to connect with those who share the same hobby, regardless of background or experience level.

Feature

Community directory with group profiles that members can contribute to by posting events.

Card 2

Users want clear guidelines and moderation that promote respectful, welcoming conversations.

Feature

Automated content filters and clear community guidelines displayed prominently on every page.

Card 3

Users need accessible design that accommodates diverse abilities and devices.

Feature

Multi-device compatibility and text-to-speech tools for accessibility.

Value3: Passion-Driven Engagement

Card 1

Users want to join discussions that reflect their genuine interests, not just trending topics.

Feature

Personalized content feed based on interests and activity, filtering out irrelevant trends.

Card 2

Users need events or content that reward and recognize dedication over popularity.

Feature

Badges or recognition systems for active participation, skill-building, and contribution.

Value4: Safety

Card 1

Users need consistent moderation to protect against harassment and hate speech.

Feature

Real-time content monitoring with automated flagging and human moderators.

Card 2

Users need reporting tools that are simple, transparent, and lead to real action.

Feature

Easy-to-use reporting button with a clear follow-up process and updates on actions taken.
